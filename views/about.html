Tāranātha's life

Tāranātha lived from 1575 to 1634 in Tibet and was one of the most influential scholars of his time. He was not only knwon for his knowledge in Tibetan Buddhist philosophy, but also for his political influence on the Tsang rulers of his time.

His name which derives from Sanskrit shows his affinity to the language of the holy land India and his close connection to Tāra.

His philosophical works and his importance for the proclamation of Shentong or gzhan stong in Tibetan.

Tā ra nā tha is considered second in importance only to Dol po pa with regards to the proclamation of gzhan stong and the number of authored works. He clarifies and expands Dol po pa’s exegesis of gzhan stong unlike anyone before him, and he is also credited with systemizing Dol po pa’s gzhan stong view into a tenet system. This remains an important issue in Tibetan philosophical history, and a study of Tā ra nā tha’s dBu ma theg mchog will reveal a deeper understanding of this development.

The persecution of the Jo nang school which began in the 17th century was for the largest part due to political circumstances and the need of the dGe lugs pas to get rid of a powerful political enemy. Tā ra nā tha had enjoyed a close relationship with the different Tsangpa rulers of his time, and when these were defeated by Gushri Khan’s Qoshot Mongols acting in favor of the dGe lugs pas, the Jo nang monasteries were converted to the dGe lugs curriculum. Thus, in Central Tibet the works and printing blocks of the Jo nang school were sealed, reprints were forbidden, and the tradition survived only in A mdo, where the legislative arm of the dGe lugs government could not reach, overlooked by most Tibet-ans outside that area. The reprinting of Tā ra nā tha’s works was only allowed again in the 19th centu-ry.
