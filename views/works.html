Tāranātha's works

Tā ra nā tha’s collected works (gsung ’bum) are preserved in different editions whose volumes number anywhere from 10 to 45. His compositions range from tantric commentarial literature to autobio-graphical works , pilgrimage literature , an analysis of the dohās of different Indian siddhas , histor-ical works and philosophical works. His philosophical works number to around ten and are mostly found in the final volumes of his gsung ’bum. They mostly explain his Empty-of-Other Great Madh-yamaka (gzhan stong dbu ma chen po), but also comment on the Prajñāpāramitā literature (in which he also endorses his gzhan stong view). Therefore, apart from his numerous works on tantra, his phil-osophical works are few.

The dBu ma theg mchog is the most detailed one and elaborates on all essen-tial points of his gzhan stong view. Besides this, his disciple has written down explanations by Tā ra nā tha himself, and this commentary clarifies the root text to a considerable degree. The dBu ma theg mchog is considered to be “essential for understanding the zhentong presentation by the great Jonang scholar Taranatha.”

The text is divided into eight chapters and consists of 53 folios in the rTag brtan Phun tshogs gling edition or 53 pages in the Jo nang dpe tshogs edition published in 2007. It covers a wide range of topics relevant to the gzhan stong view: the nature of the middle, buddha-essence (here equated to the dharmadhātu), the eight consciousnesses, the three natures, dependent arising, the two selflessnesses, the two truths, path and result as well as a definition of the Five Dharmas in chapter five, which are taken from the Laṅkāvatārasūtra. All these topics are essential Mahāyāna concepts ranging from Yogācāra to Madhyamaka and from the Laṅkāvatārasūtra to the Tathāgata-garbhasūtras.
